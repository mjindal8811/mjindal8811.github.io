About Me

Hi, I’m Mannan Jindal — a student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where I’m pursuing a double major in Computer Engineering and Computer Science. I’m deeply fascinated by how hardware and software interact to bring complex systems to life. Whether I’m writing Verilog for an FPGA or debugging a distributed program, I enjoy the process of turning abstract ideas into tangible, working results.

Outside the classroom, I love exploring new challenges and experiences that keep me balanced and creative. When I’m not coding, you’ll probably find me on the water sailing or windsurfing, or spending a quiet evening playing my guitar. These hobbies remind me of the importance of rhythm — both in music and in life.
